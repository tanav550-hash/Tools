The History of Numerology

Numerology is one of the oldest studies of numbers, dating back thousands of years. The ancient civilizations of Babylon, Egypt, Greece, and China all practiced some form of numerology.

Pythagoras, the Greek mathematician and philosopher (c. 570â€“495 BCE), is often credited with formalizing numerology in the Western world. He believed that the universe is built upon the power of numbers and that everything can be expressed in numerical terms.

In the East, numerology has been an integral part of Chinese culture for millennia, particularly in practices like Feng Shui. The Chinese system assigns different meanings to numbers than the Western system.

Modern numerology primarily uses the Pythagorean system, which assigns numbers 1-9 to the letters of the alphabet. This system was further developed in the early 20th century by L. Dow Balliett and Dr. Juno Jordan.

Numerology Calculation Methods

There are several systems for calculating numerology numbers, but the most common is the Pythagorean system:

Pythagorean Conversion Chart

1: A, J, S

2: B, K, T

3: C, L, U

4: D, M, V

5: E, N, W

6: F, O, X

7: G, P, Y

8: H, Q, Z

9: I, R

Life Path Number: Calculated from your full birth date. Add the day, month, and year separately, reduce each to a single digit or master number, then add those together and reduce again if necessary.

Destiny Number: Calculated from your full birth name. Convert each letter to its corresponding number, add them together, and reduce to a single digit or master number.

Soul Urge Number: Uses only the vowels in your name to represent your inner desires and motivations.

Personality Number: Uses only the consonants in your name to show how others perceive you.
